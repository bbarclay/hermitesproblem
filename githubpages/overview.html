Abstract

Hermite's Problem asks: What are the necessary and sufficient conditions for a cubic irrational to generate a periodic sequence? This interactive paper presents three novel, mathematically equivalent approaches—projective geometry, matrix theory, and a modified sin²-algorithm—offering a unified solution to this classical problem.

What is Hermite's Problem?

In 1848, Charles Hermite posed a fundamental question in number theory:
"How can we characterize all cubic irrationals that generate periodic sequences, analogous to the periodic continued fractions for quadratic irrationals?"

  • Quadratic irrationals always have periodic continued fractions.
  • Cubic irrationals are more subtle: only some generate periodic sequences, and the conditions are deeper.
  • This paper provides three complementary methods for complete characterization, with interactive tools for exploration.
