Three Novel Approaches to Hermite's Problem

HAPD Algorithm

The Hermite Algorithm for Periodicity Detection (HAPD) uses projective geometry to visualize and detect periodicity in cubic irrationals.

  • Leverages projective space \(\mathbb{P}^2\)
  • Geometric interpretation of periodicity
  • Handles all cubic irrationals
Learn More

Matrix Approach

Uses companion matrices and trace sequences to algebraically characterize periodicity in cubic irrationals.

  • Companion matrix encodes the polynomial
  • Trace sequence reveals modular periodicity
  • Connects to Galois theory
Learn More

Modified sin²-Algorithm

An extension of Karpenkov's algorithm, adapted for cubics with complex conjugate roots, using the squared sine of the argument.

  • Specialized for complex roots
  • Simple algebraic periodicity test
  • Algorithmic efficiency

Equivalence of Methods

Theorem: For any cubic irrational \(\alpha\), the following are equivalent:
  1. The HAPD algorithm produces a periodic sequence for \(\alpha\).
  2. The trace sequence from the matrix approach exhibits modular periodicity.
  3. The modified sin²-algorithm detects periodicity for \(\alpha\).
Each method offers a unique lens, but all lead to the same mathematical truth.
